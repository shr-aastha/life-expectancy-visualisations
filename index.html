• Factors affecting Life Expectancy and Mortality Rates

This webpage contains visualisations depicting the impact of various socio-economic and health factors on life expectancy and mortality rates.

For the visualisations, the Life Expectancy (WHO) Fixeddataset retrieved from Kaggle and the GNI data of countries from 2000 to 2015 obtained from World Bank's Data Bank were used.

Correlation between Income Levels and Life Expectancy

Data taken from Life Expectancy (WHO) Fixed and World Bank's Data Bank

The above visualisations depict the strong relationship between the economic status of a country or region and the life expectancy of the population residing within that particular country or region.

Impact of Schooling on Life Expectancy and Human Mortality

Data taken from Life Expectancy (WHO) Fixed and World Bank's Data Bank

Through the attainment of formal education, a nation's population becomes more aware of hygiene, preventative healthcare and the importance of maintaining a healthy lifestyle. The visualisations showcased above illustrate the impact of schooling on life expectancy and mortality rates.

Role of Vaccination in Enhancing Global Health

Data taken from Life Expectancy (WHO) Fixed and World Bank's Data Bank

Immunisation against fatal diseases like measles, polio, diptheria and hepatitis B plays a significant role in minimising premature deaths and enhancing life expectancy. The contribution of preventative healthcare in achieving positive health outcomes can be inferred from the visualisations presented above.
